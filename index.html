Integrating Writing and Design Portfolio

Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

My design Fuego is an introductory coding assignment where I was able to learn the basics of html to produce a brief biographical piece of work. It required that I learn several new coding-based techniques like creating a Github repository, editing prefab code (and adding my own lines), as well as figuring out how to publish my work as a usable website.

Logo A/B/C

Here I used tools such as Canva and Adobe to design logos that I felt represented me as a professional. An important aspect to highlight from this was the use of giving the sites prompts and meshing together designs they recommended with limited intervention from me. This project required understanding how to design with a computer model and how to choose visual elements that represent myself professionally.

UX Testing Logo Project

UX testing is a type of usability test where participant feedback highlights issues with accessibility and design appeal of a product or service you may be offering. Here, I took the logos created from the A/B/C assignment and asked three participants a series of questions to gain insights that led to me creating a new and improved logo that addresses the previous ones faults.

Cartoon Portrait

This cartoon project allowed me to be creative in Adobe Illustrator, using the various tools to replicate an image of myself in cartoon style. This project allowed me to discover the tips and tricks of Adobe that I can apply on future designs and use the principles for revision of past ones.

Good Design

This project was an encompassing of all the design methods and tools we have learned up to this point in the semester. Harnessing the design principles of Robin William’s book chapters and my newfound Adobe skills, I was able to explain through technical writing exactly how Joni Mitchell uses graphic design elements to make her album cover a good design.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

The technology we’ve learned to use has allowed us to create pieces of work that goes beyond aesthetic appeal and engages deeper with the principles of design. Giving us a large array of typographical and coding-based assets, technology lets us customize and connect to our projects. In the “Good Design” project for example, by incorporating technical writing with stylized rhetoric to create concise but informative text, we could convey the principles of design in impactful ways. This project also emphasized the ethics of our work and how our designs can become more accessible to others that experience the world a bit differently. By incorporating the ideas of alignment, my descriptions of the good design principles serve the purpose of feeling intentional on a page without sacrificing the usability of the design to those such as people with dyslexia who have trouble reading center-alignment paragraphs. Each of these components separately can enhance a design, but meshing them together creates more creative and thoughtful outcomes.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Williams' principle of having control over something once you can name it resonated with me deeply with the design process. Coming into this class I had next to no experience in regards to graphic design but having learned the core principles I can apply them directly to my own work. I feel that this is especially powerful when it comes to critique during workshops where having the necessary vocabulary allowed me to know how to give feeback, giving others the support to lift up their creations. Naming the main design principles was also especially important in my “Good Design” project, being able to identify and categorize the important concepts of designs, allowing me to say how the alignment of the piece creates cohesion or how the contrast is important to the visual flow.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

The ability to revise and reshape our work into the best that it can be rarely happens in isolation. The act of workshop, putting out what you have, even if it's not 100% of the way finished is imperative to the design process. During the good design workshop, I knew my technical writing needed to be honed in, but I created a basis of what I wanted and got feedback from my peers. As creators, we harbor biases towards our own work that get overlooked during the process of making, but showing others brings on fresh ideas, new perspectives, and respective critiques. It additionally fosters a space to ask for help with design, allowing us to gain support from each other in a safe environment where we create community and trust with others working on similar designs. I found it extremely helpful during the cartoon project to see what other people did for their assignments and having the ability to ask things like, “how did you get your facial expressions to convey so well over drawing”? Through these exchanges of ideas and mutual support, we become better designers in our own rites, but even more so, learn how to guide others in their design journey, just as they help us reach new levels within our own practice.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

No design is made perfect on the first go around and my work so far this semester is no exception. I have found that my most impactful revisions have been on projects such as “Good Design” where I had to revise both visual and textual elements. Stemming from skills learned during this project, it allowed me to return to past ones and employ these new insights. After learning more of InDeisign and some of the more technical skills required on Adobe, I was able to add onto my Cartoon project now that I was more comfortable with the tools. I also made use of the technical writing from “Good Design” on my UX study presentation, making sure to implement this toolset in all my work.

5. Learn to present your work.

Communication as a whole is an important skillset that I believe all people, but especially within my field of science need to be individual experts at. I thought that through presenting my UX study to my group I not only became more confident of the underlying principles of UX, but also on how to present designs to others. Other then orally, the ability to share your work from a portfolio standpoint, especially when building professional connections is imperative to success within the business sector. I found that through creating the Design Fuego and publishing it as a website, it was a first step for me to get my creative work out into the open.
